## B.C. man cured of rare disease in world-first for new gene-editing technology

## Candidate Summary
A B.C. man (Ty Sperle) became the first person cured of a rare genetic disease using prime editing technology developed by Prime Medicine. The treatment involved extracting his cells, correcting the genetic 'spelling mistake', and reinfusing them - successfully treating carbamoyl phosphate synthetase 1 (CPS1) deficiency.

## Why It Matters
This clinical success validates prime editing as a therapeutic modality and demonstrates it can cure genetic diseases that may be untreatable with other CRISPR approaches. First proof-of-concept for precise, allogeneic prime editing therapy.
