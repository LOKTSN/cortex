# Kimi K2.5: Moonshot AI's Open-Weight MoE Model with Elite Math Performance

## TL;DR

Moonshot AI has released Kimi K2.5, a 1T parameter Mixture-of-Experts model (32B active parameters) with open weights under a Modified MIT license. The model achieves a 96.1 on AIME 2025—significantly outperforming Claude's 87.2—while pricing at $0.60 per million input tokens, roughly 8x cheaper. Key differentiators include native multimodal capabilities via MoonViT-3D, 256K context length, and an Agent Swarm architecture that coordinates 100 sub-agents, delivering a substantial 18-point BrowseComp gain over single-agent setups.

## Key Insights

**Elite Math Performance at Accessible Cost**
Kimi K2.5's AIME 2025 score of 96.1 positions it as the top open-weight model for mathematical reasoning, surpassing Claude Opus 4-6 by nearly 9 points. At $0/M input tokens, it undercuts proprietary alternatives by an order of magnitude, making high-performance math inference economically viable at scale.

**Agent Swarm Architecture Enables Genuine Capability Multiplication**
The 100-sub-agent Swarm design isn't merely marketing—it produced a 78.4% BrowseComp score versus 60.6% for single-agent configurations, a 17.8-point delta. This suggests emergent coordination capabilities when multiple reasoning agents collaborate, opening avenues for complex task decomposition.

**Open Weights + Modified MIT License Enables Self-Hosting and Fine-Tuning**
Unlike fully restrictive APIs, researchers and enterprises can deploy Kimi K2.5 on-premises, fine-tune on domain-specific datasets, and retain full data sovereignty—a critical advantage for healthcare, finance, and government workloads with stringent compliance requirements.

## Why This Matters

For organizations requiring state-of-the-art math and science capabilities without vendor lock-in or prohibitive costs, Kimi K2.5 delivers an compelling open-weight alternative that combines elite performance with deployment flexibility and meaningful multi-agent orchestration.
