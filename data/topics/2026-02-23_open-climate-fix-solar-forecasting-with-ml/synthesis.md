# Open Climate Fix: ML-Powered Solar Energy Forecasting

## TL;DR

Open Climate Fix is a non-profit developing open-source ML models that predict solar energy production from satellite imagery and weather data. Their Quartz Solar Forecast model, deployed by National Grid ESO in the UK, has reduced solar forecasting errors by 50% — directly reducing the fossil fuel reserves needed to compensate for renewable energy variability.

## Key Insights

**Satellite Imagery + ML = Accurate Solar Prediction**
By analyzing real-time satellite images of cloud cover and combining them with historical generation data, Open Climate Fix's models predict solar output 0-8 hours ahead with accuracy that traditional weather-based forecasting can't match.

**Deployed at National Grid Scale**
The models are actively used by National Grid ESO to manage the UK's electricity grid. Better solar forecasts mean less gas-fired backup generation, directly reducing CO2 emissions estimated at 100,000+ tonnes per year.

**Fully Open Source Stack**
Models, training data, and inference pipelines are all open source. Any grid operator worldwide can deploy the same forecasting capabilities — a model for how climate tech can scale through open-source collaboration.

## Why This Matters

Renewable energy's biggest challenge is variability — the sun doesn't always shine, and the wind doesn't always blow. Accurate forecasting is the key to integrating more renewables into the grid without compromising reliability. Open Climate Fix proves that ML can solve this at grid scale.
