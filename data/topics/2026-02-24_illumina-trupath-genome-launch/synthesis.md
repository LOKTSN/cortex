## TL;DR

Illumina's TruPath Genome is a new whole genome sequencing (WGS) tool that achieves unprecedented accuracy in previously inaccessible "dark regions" of the genome, processes 16 genomes per day with only 10 minutes hands-on time, and costs $395 per genome—nearly doubling throughput compared to competing long-read methods while dramatically reducing complexity and cost.

## Key Insights

**1. Breaking the dark region barrier.** Traditional short-read sequencing struggles to reliably detect variants in repetitive or structurally complex genomic regions—often called "dark regions." TruPath leverages advanced computational algorithms and optimized chemistry to resolve these challenging areas, potentially unlocking diagnostic answers for patients with rare diseases who previously received inconclusive results.

**2. A workflow designed for scale.** The system delivers 16 whole genomes per day (nearly 2× the throughput of competing long-read platforms) with just 10 minutes of hands-on time. At $395 per genome, the pricing represents a significant reduction from historical WGS costs, making comprehensive testing more accessible for clinical laboratories and health systems.

**3. Clinical adoption potential.** Rare disease research has long been constrained by the inability to detect variants in inaccessible genomic regions. By combining high accuracy with a streamlined, cost-effective workflow, TruPath could accelerate the transition from targeted panels or exomes to comprehensive whole genome testing in routine clinical practice.

**Why this matters:** This breakthrough could enable broader clinical adoption of comprehensive WGS, ultimately providing more patients with rare diseases a definitive molecular diagnosis.
