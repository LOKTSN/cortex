## Candidate Summary
UC Berkeley researchers (led by Jennifer Doudna) developed NANITE, a virus-like CRISPR delivery system that replicates and spreads to neighboring cells. The self-spreading editor achieved ~3x higher editing efficiency in cell culture and significantly reduced disease protein levels in mice at lower doses than standard CRISPR.

## Why It Matters
This represents a conceptual shift in therapeutic delivery - the ability to spread beyond initially edited cells could enable lower dosing, reduced immune responses, and treatment of tissues previously difficult to reach with gene therapy.
