## TL;DR

Isomorphic Labs has unveiled IsoDDE, a unified computational drug-design engine that more than doubles AlphaFold 3's accuracy on protein-ligand structure prediction, surpasses gold-standard physics-based methods (FEP+) for binding affinity prediction, and can identify novel binding pockets from amino acid sequence alone—representing a major leap from structure prediction toward end-to-end drug design.

## Key Insights

**1. Structural accuracy breakthrough.** IsoDDE more than doubles AlphaFold 3's performance on protein-ligand structure prediction benchmarks. This isn't incremental improvement—it's a step-change in how accurately the system can model how small molecules (ligands) fit into their target protein structures, a critical foundation for rational drug design.

**2. Binding affinity at scale.** The engine outperforms FEP+ (free energy perturbation), widely considered the gold standard for physics-based binding affinity prediction. This is significant because binding affinity—how strongly a drug candidate binds to its target—directly predicts therapeutic efficacy. Achieving this with a machine learning model rather than expensive compute-heavy simulations opens the door to screening far more compounds early in the pipeline.

**3. Novel pocket discovery from sequence.** Perhaps most innovatively, IsoDDE can identify previously unknown binding pockets using only the amino acid sequence of a protein target. Traditionally, drug hunters relied on known structures or experimental data to find where a drug could bind. This capability could unlock "undruggable" targets and expand the therapeutic landscape.

## Why this matters

By combining structure prediction, affinity estimation, and pocket identification in a single system, IsoDDE moves AI drug discovery closer to a true design engine—potentially compressing lead optimization timelines and reducing costly experimental cycles.
